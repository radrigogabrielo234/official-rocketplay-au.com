Signs of Gambling Problems

While gambling can be a fun and entertaining activity, it can become problematic if not managed properly. Here are some signs of potential gambling problems:

  • Spending more money than you intended: If you find yourself gambling more than you can afford, or exceeding your financial limits, it may be a sign of a problem.

  • Gambling to escape problems: Using gambling as a way to cope with stress, emotional issues, or personal problems is a serious concern.

  • Inability to stop gambling: If you feel that you cannot stop gambling, even if you want to, it may indicate a problem.

  • Negative impact on relationships: Gambling can strain relationships with friends, family, and colleagues.

  • Chasing losses: If you try to win back money you’ve lost, it’s a warning sign of a potentially unhealthy gambling habit.

If you or someone you know exhibits any of these signs, we encourage you to seek support and consider implementing self-exclusion or setting limits.

Safe Gambling Recommendations

We recommend the following tips to promote safe gambling:

  • Set a budget: Always decide beforehand how much you are willing to spend and stick to it. Do not gamble more than you can afford to lose.

  • Set time limits: Allocate a specific time for gambling and take regular breaks. Don’t gamble for extended periods.

  • Avoid chasing losses: Accept that losses are part of the game. Never try to win back lost money, as it can lead to deeper financial problems.

  • Recognize when it’s time to stop: If you’re feeling frustrated, anxious, or upset, take a break from gambling. If you feel that gambling is no longer fun, it’s time to stop.

  • Seek help when needed: If you ever feel your gambling habits are getting out of control, seek assistance from support services or professional help.
